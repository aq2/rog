~~
something nasty in the woodshed
~~

The band's story started in 1992, with Rog Hunt (guitar) and Julie Matthews (vocals and flute). Rog had hardly sung and mainly knew 70s/80s rock and post punk (with a bit of Dylan and Nick Cave thrown in). Julie, having run the folk club at Swansea Uni, was heavily skilled in folk songs - both vocally and on the flute.

Rog realised that he was better at writing strange chord guitar shenanigans to Julie's lyrics than teaching, so the pair of them hacked up to East Devon from Brixham, with Rog leaving teaching for the good of all.

They started attending a few Folk Nights, roughly performing folk songs learned from Julie's books. It was a good breeding ground - things had to tighten up whilst playing live to a bunch of strangers! Early sessions also featured a few self-penned numbers with Matthew Smith (briefly on bass and recording), Ian Richardson (keys) and Nick Martin playing fiddle and electric guitar.

This got the attention of a local recording studio, Daylight Music. Ian Dent recorded our first session and put The Mirror (lyrics/tune by Julie, rest by Rog) on a compilation of 24 Devon bands, with the risqué coastal-rock title Long and Pink!

By the time the CD was released, the line-up had changed, with Matthew gone to Australia. The others advertised in local papers and music stores (no social media then!), and were soon gifted replies from Chris Durant (a tidy drummer) and Rob Hills, a talented Sidmothian bass player. We began rehearsing new songs co-written by Rog (who had begun exploring ways to make songs from a loose imagination and the most unusual chords in his guitar book) and Julie's tune-smithery and writing skills.

But we had to find A NAME, always a brain-teaser for any band. Rog had recently enjoyed Stella Gibbons' strange and comic novel Cold Comfort Farm, featuring the Starkadders and the 'Something Nasty in the Woodshed' quote. The name has mostly stuck in some form, even to this day! Before long, weird monster-headed t-shirts were in production…

Something Nasty got lucky, somehow making contact with Exeter's Cavern Club who needed a band to test the venue's acoustics and mixing desk! Often rehearsing twice a week, the songs came a-flowing and gigs were found. The former 'Sleeper' in Seaton was a favourite with locals who knew us, filling the place good and rowdy.

All good things come to an end though and, without ever discovering what was ultimately unidentifiable in the woodshed, Something Nasty reached a signing-off time in mid 1995.

Luckily, through the magic of the interwebs, their songs are still accessible on the right hand side of this very page!
